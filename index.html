What is PM-Uday Scheme?

PM Uday Scheme stands for Prime Ministers Unauthorized Colonies in Delhi Awas Adhikar Yojana. It was passed by the Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha of the Parliament to facilitate regulations of the colonies and handing over the ownership rights of the properties to the anxious residents. PM Uday awas adhikar yojana is a step towards ensuring dignity in the lives of thousands of families.

You must be thinking about how you can get the ownership rights of your flat or home if you are living in an unauthorized colony in Delhi. Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is PM-UDAY?

    PM-UDAY (Prime Minister â€“ Unauthorized Colonies in Delhi Awas Adhikar Yojana) Scheme is a decision of Union Cabinet to confer the ownership or transfer/ mortgage rights to the residents of Unauthorized Colonies in Delhi.

  • What are the benefits under the PM-UDAY Scheme?

    The PM-UDAY scheme is for conferment of property rights to the residents of 1731 Unauthorized Colonies (UCs) in Delhi. The regularization of UCsentails approval of layout plans of the colony, which in turn requires fire clearances of the colony and the properties therein from Department of Delhi Fire Services, Govt of NCT of Delhi.

    The conferment of property rights for Govt / Pvt Land under PM-UDAY will allow the residents of UCs the following:

    1-  The residents can sale/purchase their properties without any hindrance.
    2-   Residents can avail loans from banks / financial institutions against their properties.
    3-  Residents can get building plans approved if they want to change anything in the present building or construct house in the vacant plot for whichthey have got property rights.

    Further, conferment of rights opens up huge possibility of redevelopment. DDA willcome out with relaxed development control norms and incentives in terms of higher Floor Area Ratio (FAR) in these colonies so that new development can take place with much wider roads, green areas, public spaces like parks etc., adequate infrastructure in terms of water, sewer, sanitation etc., social amenities like schools, hospitals, entertainment etc. This will bring ease in life of citizens and improve their quality of lives.

    Therefore, conferment of rights will give far more to the residents of UCs thanmere regularization, for name sake.

  • How many colonies have been covered by the notified Regulations?

    The notified Regulations dt. 29.10.2019 covers 1731 Unauthorized Colonies (UCs) existing as on 01.06.2014 provided they have minimum 50% built-up area as on 01.01.2015 identified by Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi.

  • How many UCs are excluded in these Regulations?

    The Regulation excludes 69 Affluent Unauthorized Colonies.

  • What are documents required for conferment of ownership rights underPM UDAY Scheme.

    Any resident of any unauthorized colony having registered or un-registered or notarized Power of Attorney, Agreement to Sale, Will, possession letter and other documents including evidencing payment of consideration shall be eligible for right ofownership or transfer or mortgage through a conveyance deed or authorization slip, as the case may be.
